<h2 class="topic-title">MS DHONI</h2>

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as MS Dhoni, is a legendary Indian cricketer. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India, he is a right-handed batsman and wicket-keeper. Dhoni is widely regarded as one of the greatest finishers in cricket history. He made his international debut in 2004 and quickly rose to fame with his exceptional skills. Dhoni's calm and composed nature on the field earned him the nickname "Captain Cool". Under his leadership, India won the ICC World Cup in 2011, defeating Sri Lanka in the final. He is also the first captain to win all three ICC trophies: World Cup, Champions Trophy, and T20 World Cup. Dhoni's impressive career includes 10773 runs in ODIs and 4876 runs in Tests. He has scored 10 Test centuries and 73 ODI fifties. Dhoni's exceptional wicket-keeping skills have earned him 256 catches and 123 stumpings in ODIs. He has been awarded the ICC ODI Player of the Year award three times. Dhoni has also been named the captain of the ICC World Test XI and ICC ODI XI multiple times. In 2015, he announced his retirement from Test cricket. Dhoni's leadership skills and strategic thinking have inspired many young cricketers. He is known for his ability to remain calm under pressure and make bold decisions. Dhoni's love for the Indian Army is well-known, and he has been conferred with the honorary rank of Lieutenant Colonel. He has also been involved in various philanthropic activities, including supporting the education of underprivileged children. Dhoni's biopic, "M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story", was a huge success in 2016. He is married to Sakshi Singh Dhoni and has a daughter named Ziva. Dhoni's legacy continues to inspire and influence the world of cricket. He will always be remembered as one of the greatest cricketers of all time.
